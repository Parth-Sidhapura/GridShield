# Attack Analysis

## False Data Injection Attack (FDIA)

FDIA involves the deliberate manipulation of sensor measurements to alter the perceived state of the power system. Unlike simple anomalies, FDIA is designed to remain undetected by conventional monitoring systems.

By injecting carefully crafted false data, attackers can:
- Mislead state estimation algorithms
- Trigger incorrect control actions
- Mask actual faults in the system

## Load Altering Attack (LAA)

LAA targets the demand side of the grid by artificially modifying load patterns. This can be achieved by compromising smart meters or demand-response systems.

Impact includes:
- Frequency instability
- Voltage fluctuations
- Overloading of transmission lines

## Command Injection Attacks

In this type of attack, malicious actors send unauthorized control commands to grid components such as circuit breakers or transformers.

Potential consequences:
- Sudden shutdown of critical infrastructure
- Cascading failures across the grid
- Physical damage to equipment

## Overall Impact

These attacks are particularly dangerous because they bridge the gap between cyber systems and physical infrastructure, making them cyber-physical in nature.

The lack of built-in security mechanisms in legacy protocols significantly increases the attack surface.
